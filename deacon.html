Deacon Gregory Sherman

Deacon Gregory S. Sherman was ordained a Transitional Deacon on December 10, 1995 at Holy Name of Jesus Catholic Church in West Palm. He has continuously served as a Permanent Deacon in The Diocese of Palm Beach for the past 27 years at several parishes. He was awarded a Masters of Divinity (M.Div., 1993) and a Masters of Theology (M.Thy., 1994) by St. Vincent DePaul Regional Seminary (SVDP)in Boynton Beach.

Deacon Greg taught school in the Diocese of Palm Beach: Cardinal Newman HS (combined 11 years); Rosarian Academy (2 years); Holy Name Of Jesus (1 Year). While at Cardinal Newman HS, Deacon Greg was its golf coach first Junior Varsity and then the State acclaimed Varsity Golf Team including the PGA's World-ranked Brooks Koepka.

Deacon Greg is currently a full-time Interfaith Chaplain with VITAS Health Care and Hospice and has been for the past 11 years. He resides in Boynton Beach.

His parents and family were very important to him in his early years in his Catholic faith and tradition. The middle child of seven siblings of the late Robert W. Sherman, JR, and Ellen Marie Sherman who were founding members of St. Juliana Parish in West Palm Beach.

Deacon Greg and his family first met Rev. Michael O'Flarity more than forty years ago at St. Juliana and have remained friends with Fr. O'Flarity ever since. Deacon Greg also met Rev. Dominic Toan Tran at St. Juliana and Rev. Martin Deveraux during his Seminary years at SVDP.

Prior to his Seminary days, Deacon Greg graduated from the University of Florida (1973) with a Bachelor of Science, in Business Administration (marketing and finance). Following graduation from UF, he worked for three “Fortune 50 companies” in a variety of sales and marketing positions.

Deacon Greg enjoys golf, cooking and eating, gardening, traveling, reading, listening to jazz and Classical music, and visiting family and friends.

Deacon Greg, “I am humbled and honored by serving Fr. Dominic and the parishioner's of St. John the Evangelist and to be a part of this community. I look forward to continuing to serve through my Diaconal ministry.”
